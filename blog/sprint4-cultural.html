Neuroplasticity

The medical definition of neuroplasticity predominantly deals with the brain's ability to repair itself in the case of an accident. If you were to suffer a head injury, the brain will reorganise neural pathways to preserve or relearn some of what was stored in the damaged neurons.

Understanding neuroplasticity

A broader description of Neuroplasticity is the brain's capacity to continually change over the course of your life. In the past it had been thought that there were some things that once learned could not be altered. We have all heard the saying “you can’t teach an old dog a new trick”, well it turns out to be incorrect. In fact you can teach a dog new tricks, also your co-workers:

The brain has the ability to learn new things and change perspectives on fixed opinions by conditioning through training or new experience.
